How to Wear a Suit

A suit is a coordinated set of formal clothing typically worn for business, formal events, or professional settings. It generally consists of a jacket and trousers made from matching fabric, and sometimes includes a matching vest (three-piece suit). Suits are designed to create a polished, cohesive look that is both stylish and structured, providing a sleek, tailored appearance.

How to wear a Suit

Instructions:
  • Ensure a Proper Fit:
    1. The shoulders should fit snugly without overextending or creating a gap.
    2. Sleeves should end at the wrist bone, allowing about 1/4 to 1/2 inch of the shirt cuff to show.
    3. The jacket hem should cover the seat and reach around the middle of your hand when arms are at your sides.
    4. Pants should sit comfortably at the waist without needing a belt for support. The length should have a slight break (fold) above the shoe or be hemmed for a no-break, modern look.
  • Buttoning the Jacket:
    1. Fasten only the top button when standing, and always unbutton it when sitting.
    2. Fasten only the middle button, or the top two if preferred. Always unbutton all buttons when seated.
    3. Button all buttons that are designed to be fastened, typically two or three on the side. Double-breasted jackets remain buttoned when seated.
  • Coordinate Colors and Patterns:
    1. For a timeless look, go for neutral colors like navy, charcoal, or black for formal settings. Lighter colors or patterned fabrics (e.g., pinstripes or plaids) are great for business-casual or social occasions.
    2. Ensure your shirt contrasts with the suit (a white or light blue shirt is versatile). The tie should coordinate with both the suit and shirt; choose complementary colors or patterns that don’t overwhelm the look.
  • Accesorize Appropriately:
    1. Tie: The width of the tie should match the width of your jacket lapels. Choose a tie length that reaches just above your belt buckle.
    2. Pocket Square: Adds sophistication; it should complement but not exactly match your tie.
    3. Tie Clip: Place it between the third and fourth buttons of your shirt, ensuring it’s no wider than the tie.
    4. Belt and Shoes: Always match your belt and shoe colors for cohesion. Black, brown, or oxblood are standard choices.
  • Additional Tips:
    1. Check the Lapel Style: Notched lapels are the most versatile, while peak lapels are more formal, and shawl lapels are often seen on tuxedos or dinner jackets.
    2. Layer a Vest: For a three-piece suit, the vest should fit snugly and cover the waistband. Leave the bottom button of the vest unbuttoned for a traditional touch.
