How to Wear a Scarf

A scarf worn with a professional outfit, like a suit, adds a layer of style, sophistication, and sometimes warmth. For formal wear, scarves are typically lightweight and made from fine materials like silk, cashmere, or high-quality wool, keeping the look polished. When draped around the neck, they can add a subtle color or pattern that complements the suit without overwhelming it.

Four ways to wear a Scarf

Instructions:
  • The Drape

    This is the simplest and most traditional style, often seen with professional attire.

    1. Drape the scarf evenly around your neck, letting each end fall in front.
    2. Adjust the length so the scarf rests just above the suitâ€™s buttons.
  • The Once-Around

    This style offers a bit more coverage and is excellent for cooler settings.

    1. Place the scarf around your neck, leaving one end slightly longer than the other.
    2. Take the longer end and wrap it around your neck once, letting both ends hang in front.
  • The French Knot

    A stylish, polished option, perfect for a professional setting.

    1. Fold the scarf in half lengthwise, creating a loop at one end.
    2. Place the folded scarf around your neck with the loop on one side.
    3. Pull the loose ends through the loop and adjust for comfort and tightness.
  • The Reverse Drape Cross

    This is a more secure style that stays in place and offers extra warmth.

    1. Drape the scarf around your neck with one end longer than the other.
    2. Wrap the longer end around your neck and bring it back to the front.
    3. Tuck both ends neatly, letting them fall at even lengths in front.
